Types of Car Ceramic Coating

Types of Car Ceramic Coating


Ceramic coating is a relatively new way of protecting your car's exterior from scratches, dings and dirt. It's becoming increasingly popular among car owners because it offers an extra layer of protection that conventional waxes and sealants can't match! There are two main types of ceramic coatings; liquid and powder. Liquid coating is applied on the paint directly as a thin film and then cured with a UV light after application. Powder coatings are applied onto the surface as a dry powder that needs to be heated in order to adhere onto the surface.

No matter which type you choose, both offer superior protection compared to traditional waxing techniques. Liquid coatings provide more flexibility for those who want to customize their cars by changing colors or creating unique designs with special effects such as metallic flake, chameleon or matte finishes. Powder coatings on the other hand provide excellent scratch resistance and durability but require more effort when it comes to cleaning them up afterwards.

In addition to these two types of ceramic coating there are also hybrid options available which combine the best features of liquid and powder coatings into one product such as scratch resistance with easy-to-clean properties. However, they tend to be much pricier than either of the two basic types so unless you really need those extra features its probably better off sticking with either liquid or powder coating alone!

Overall, ceramic coating products have become quite popular over recent years due to their effectiveness in protecting cars from damage and keeping them looking brand new for longer periods of time!
