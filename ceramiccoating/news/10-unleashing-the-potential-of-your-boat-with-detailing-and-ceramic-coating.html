Types of Ceramic Coatings


Ceramic coatings are a great way to unleash the potential of your boat! They provide an extra layer of protection from the elements, making it easier to keep your boat looking its best. (There are several types of ceramic coatings available, each offering various levels of protection and shine.) For example, there's self-cleaning and hydrophobic coating which repels water and dirt, while still maintaining a glossy finish. It's also UV-resistant and can withstand saltwater exposure without damage. Additionally, you have a high gloss ceramic coating which creates a "mirror-like" look that is both scratch resistant and long lasting. And lastly, there's thermal barrier coating which helps reduce heat absorption on hot surfaces, making them cooler to the touch!

Another great advantage of using these ceramic coatings is their longevity; most will last up to five years with proper maintenance. Plus they are relatively easy to apply yourself or hire someone who specializes in detailing boats. You may even decide to combine different types of ceramics for added protection and shine!

Applying a Ceramic Coating


Unleashing the potential of your boat with detailing and ceramic coating can be a game-changer! It adds a layer of protection that will keep your boat looking its best, no matter how much time you spend out on the water. (Detailing) involves deep cleaning and polishing every inch of your boat to bring back its original luster and shine. Ceramic coating is an extra step that takes it one step further, providing long-lasting protection against UV rays, corrosion, saltwater damage, and more.

Applying a ceramic coating to your boat is fairly simple. First, you'll need to clean the surface thoroughly with soap and water. This ensures the ceramic coating has something smooth to adhere to; if there's dirt or debris present, it won't bond properly. Once the area is clear of any dirt or grime, spray on the ceramic coating in thin layers until you've covered all exposed surfaces. Allow each coat to dry completely before applying another layer - this could take up to 24 hours between coats depending on ambient temperature and humidity levels.

Finally, once all coats have been applied correctly and dried fully (and evenly!), your vessel will be ready for use - albeit with a brand new protective barrier! The great thing about applying a ceramic coating is that not only does it protect against weathering elements like sun exposure and saltwater damage but also makes maintenance easier too - less time spent scrubbing away dirt & grime means more time out enjoying yourself! All in all, adding a ceramic coating can add years onto your vessel's life expectancy while keeping her looking as good as new!
